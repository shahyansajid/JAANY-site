General Scholarship

2014 General Scholarship Guidelines

Since 1971, The Japanese American Association of New York, Inc. has awarded scholarships to graduating high school students of Japanese descent living in the tri-state area who are applying to United States institutions of higher learning.

Scholarships range from $1,000.00 to $6,000.00 and include a special award honoring former Ambassador Nishimiya. We are pleased that this year, All Nippon Airways is an official sponsor and will provide two economy class roundtrip tickets to be awarded to certain scholarship recipients.

The JAA Scholarship Committee will review the applications, and the awards will be presented at the Annual Scholarship Dinner which is scheduled to be held in late May or early June. Our keynote speaker will be announced later.

Requirements:

  • A completed application form with one passport size photo.
  • A double-spaced essay not exceed 500 words on one of the following topics:
    1. 2014, Japan is Back! As a person of Japanese descent, how do you hope to contribute to our global society? Do you see yourself in public service in the future; i.e. running for office, working in diplomacy, etc. Please be specific.
    2. What new "dream" projects or future technology would you like to see the United States and Japan work together on.
    3. Tokyo Olympics 2020--what type of exciting world do you foresee for the United States and Japan 2020 and beyond.
  • Your latest school transcript AND a copy of SAT or ACT scores.
  • A letter of recommendation from your school counselor or teacher.
  • Membership in JAA. If no one in your family is a member of JAA, a membership form is available at www.jaany.org . Please call the JAA office with any questions.
  • You must attend a United States college in the fall of 2014.

Submission Instructions:

Please submit your application through our form (below) or download the PDF and submit it to:

JAA Scholarship Committee
Japanese American Association of New York, Inc.
15 West 44th Street, 11th Floor
New York, NY 10036
e-mail address: info@jaany.org
Fax: 212-840-0616

Applications may be sent via E-mail/FAX. However, letters of recommendation and transcripts, if sent electronically, must be sent from the issuer (not student applicant) and followed by original signed/stamped copies by mail.

INCOMPLETE APPLICATIONS WILL NOT BE REVIEWED.

All Applicants will be notified of the results of their application.

If you have any questions, please call us at 212-840-6942.
